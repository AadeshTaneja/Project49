Interesting Facts about Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Interesting Facts about Pigeons

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities

Interesting Facts about Camel

  • Camels have two rows of thick eyelashes to protect their eyes from the desert dust
  • Camels’ ears are small and hairy. However their sense of hearing is also extremely strong
  • A thirsty camel can drink up to 135 liters in one sitting

Interesting Facts about cicada

  • Cicadas can reach 0.79 to 3 inches in length.
  • Cicadas spend most of their life under the ground.
  • Cicadas are one of the loudest insects on the planet. They produce sound of up to 120 dB (louder than rock concert) that can be heard 0.5 miles away.

Interesting Facts about Panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.

Interesting Facts about Teddy-Bear

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.
